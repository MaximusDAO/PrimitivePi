Primitive Pi Estimation

This is how a caveman may have estimated π. When sticks of length L are randomly dropped on a surface with parallel lines spaced 2L apart, the probability of a stick crossing a line is exactly 1/π.

Therefore, if we drop many sticks and divide the total number by those crossing lines, we get an approximation of π.
